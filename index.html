🫙 Pesto Production Estimate

At peak (June–July), 18 mature basil plants can yield 15–40 cups of leaves per week. One batch of pesto uses ~2 packed cups. That's 8–20 freezer pesto portions per week at peak production.

Beat the bolt: In Decatur's summer heat, basil bolts by July. Pinch flower buds immediately. Round 2 AeroGarden plants arrive just as Round 1 declines.

Month by month2026 Season Timeline
Early April (Now)
Direct sow okra (soak seeds 12–24 hrs first, soil 65°F+). Harden off tomato transplants. AeroGarden basil sprouting. Start cucumbers in 2nd hydro unit for Bed B. Apply 2–3" straw mulch to beds after planting.
Mid April
Direct sow Blue Lake beans (Bed C) and Stringless Green beans (Bed B) after last frost (~Apr 15). Transplant pepper starts to Bed B. Direct sow cucumbers to Bed B trellis (or transplant from hydro).
Late April
Transplant AeroGarden basil: 3 to Bed A south end, 1 per tomato/pepper bag. Plant tomato starts in Bed A — all varieties including San Marzano and Amish Paste.
Mid May
Start AeroGarden basil Round 2 for succession. Watch first-round basil for flower buds — pinch immediately. Transition 2nd hydro from cucumbers → tatsoi + mustard greens.
June
Peak early summer growth. Tomato staking and training. Pinch basil continuously. First harvests: beans, cucumbers, peppers.
July — August
Peak tomato production — harvest heavily for jam making. Freeze pesto batches weekly. Okra hitting full stride. Round 2 basil transplants replacing bolted Round 1.
